For the **AWS Certified Cloud Practitioner (CLF-C02)** exam, you need to understand that the **AWS Cloud Adoption Framework (CAF)** is a strategic guide to help organizations move to the cloud by identifying "capability gaps."

The exam typically tests your ability to match specific **Perspectives** to their **Stakeholders** or **Capabilities**.

---

## 1. The 6 Perspectives of AWS CAF

AWS CAF organizes its guidance into six perspectives. A helpful way to remember them is by grouping them into **Business-focused** and **Technical-focused** categories.

### **Business-Focused Perspectives**

These focus on the "Why" and the "Who" of the organization.

|**Perspective**|**Focus Area**|**Common Stakeholders**|
|---|---|---|
|**Business**|Ensures cloud investments align with business goals (ROI, budget).|CEO, CFO, COO, CTO|
|**People**|Bridges the gap between technology and people (training, culture change).|HR, Training Managers, CIO|
|**Governance**|Orchestrates cloud initiatives while managing risk and compliance.|CIO, CTO, CFO, CRO (Risk Officer)|

### **Technical-Focused Perspectives**

These focus on the "How" and the "What" of the cloud infrastructure.

|**Perspective**|**Focus Area**|**Common Stakeholders**|
|---|---|---|
|**Platform**|Architecture and patterns for the cloud (hybrid, cloud-native).|CTO, Architects, Engineers|
|**Security**|Ensuring confidentiality, integrity, and availability of data.|CISO, Compliance Officers|
|**Operations**|Managing health and monitoring of services (SLA, incident management).|Ops Managers, Site Reliability Engineers (SRE)|

---

## 2. The 4 Phases of the Transformation Journey

While the perspectives are the "bread and butter" of the exam, you should also know the four iterative phases of a cloud transformation:

1. **Envision:** Identify and prioritize transformation opportunities.
    
2. **Align:** Identify capability gaps across the 6 perspectives.
    
3. **Launch:** Deliver pilot projects to demonstrate value.
    
4. **Scale:** Expand successful pilots to enterprise-wide production.
    

---

## 3. CAF vs. Well-Architected Framework

This is a common "trick" on the exam. Make sure you can distinguish the two:

- **AWS CAF:** A **strategic** framework for the whole **organization** to prepare for cloud adoption (People, Business, Governance).
    
- **AWS Well-Architected Framework:** A **technical** framework for **architecting specific workloads** (Performance, Cost, Reliability).
    

---

## 💡 Quick Exam Tips

- **Keywords for "People":** Look for "culture," "training," "hiring," or "upskilling."
    
- **Keywords for "Governance":** Look for "compliance," "risk management," or "program management."
    
- **Stakeholder Matching:** If a question asks who is responsible for the **Business Perspective**, the answer is usually a C-level executive like the **CFO**.
    
- **Goal:** The primary goal of CAF is to **reduce risk** and **accelerate business outcomes**.
    

Since you’re already a full-stack and AI engineer, you’ll likely find the **Platform** and **Operations** perspectives very intuitive—just focus on memorizing the stakeholders for the Business/People/Governance side!
